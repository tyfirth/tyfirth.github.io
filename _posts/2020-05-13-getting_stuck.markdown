Why did I get into software engineering? 

I keep asking myself the same question, when I'm plugging along in a lab and all of the sudden it gives me the instructions to complete and I don't even know where to begin. "What did I get myself into? Am I in over my head? Did I make a wrong decision?" The door of opportunity is closing behind me and for a moment that stretches to eternity I feel trapped, stuck in between decisions, which way should I go? The clock keeps ticking and I haven't made a move. F#@%! 

And I freeze

And I'm analyzing, comparing paths, weighing options, predicting futures. 
And I'm starting to realize there is a way out; a way forward. I'm not trapped after all.  I just needed to give myself a moment to linger in being "stuck." Embrace the problem in order to find the solution; the way forward. 

I have a feeling that much of this course will involve being "stuck." This is a feeling I've gotten more or less used to over the years when settings goals that make me stretch and reach for what I want. Whether it was deciding to go back to school and challenging myself to take on a major like physics, or learning a trade like sales, or taking on a software engineering boot camp, getting stuck and struggling to break free was part of the whole process of learning and growing. At some point, we all have to learn to like the pain just a little bit. And learning something new whether its a new concept or a skill is a reward in itself. Like most everyone, I feel the amount I learn is directly proportional to the level of challenge of the task. That all being said, while this course will certainly be challenging I feel that it will be very much worth it, especially when what I've learned will open new doors for me and allow me to pursue a new career doing what I love to do: Getting stuck!
